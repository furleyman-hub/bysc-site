Coach Vincent brings extraordinary credentials and a deep commitment to youth development to his role as Director of Coaching at BYSC. Born and raised in Kenya, Vincent went on to play professionally for Kenya Breweries Football Club, where he served as captain, before earning over 90 international caps with the Harambee Stars, Kenya's national team, including World Cup qualifying appearances for both the 1994 USA and 1998 France tournaments.

Vincent came to the United States on a soccer scholarship and went on to win two NAIA National Championships at Lindsey Wilson College, earning All-American honors and recognition as Northeast Defender of the Year. He later transferred to Southern Connecticut State University, where he was named a First Team All-American, and earned his Bachelor of Arts degree. He holds a master's in education from Manhattan College and currently serves as Athletic Director at Rippowam Cisqua School in Bedford, NY.

Off the field, Vincent is the founder and Chairperson of The Kwarula Society for Kenyan Education, a 501(c)(3) nonprofit dedicated to improving educational opportunities for children in Kenya. His life's work, both on the pitch and in the community, reflects his belief that every child has the potential to achieve great things — and that vision drives everything he brings to BYSC.
